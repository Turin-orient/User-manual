# Logging Out

This guide explains how to safely log out of Neurond Assistant to protect your account and data.

## Purpose

Learn the proper way to log out of Neurond Assistant and understand why logging out is important for security.

## Who Should Log Out

‚úÖ **Everyone should log out when:**
- Finishing work on a shared or public computer
- Leaving your workstation unattended for extended periods
- Switching to a different user account
- Experiencing technical issues (logout and re-login can fix problems)

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Method 1: Standard Logout (Recommended)

**1. Locate Your Profile Icon**
- Look at the **top-right corner** of the screen
- Find your profile picture or initial circle
- You may see your name next to it

[Screenshot: Profile icon in top-right corner highlighted]

**2. Click the Profile Icon**
- Click once on your profile picture/icon
- A dropdown menu will appear

[Screenshot: Profile dropdown menu open]

**3. Select "Logout" or "Sign Out"**
- In the dropdown menu, look for:
  - "Logout"
  - "Sign Out"
  - "Log Off"
- Click this option

[Screenshot: Logout option in dropdown menu highlighted]

**4. Confirm Logout (if prompted)**
- Some systems ask "Are you sure you want to log out?"
- Click "Yes" or "Confirm"

[Screenshot: Logout confirmation dialog]

**5. Wait for Redirect**
- You'll be redirected to the login page
- This confirms you're logged out successfully

---

### Method 2: Direct Logout Link (if available)

Some organizations provide a direct logout link.

**Steps:**
1. Look for a "Logout" link in the top navigation bar
2. Click the link
3. Wait for redirect to login page

[Screenshot: Direct logout link in navigation]

## Expected Result

### Successful Logout Looks Like

‚úÖ **You've logged out successfully when you see:**
- The **login page** appears
- Your session data is cleared
- The URL may change to `/login` or `/auth`
- No personal information is visible on screen

[Screenshot: Login page after successful logout]

### What Happens to Your Work

**Auto-Save Feature:**
- ‚úÖ All conversations are **automatically saved**
- ‚úÖ Upload history is **preserved**
- ‚úÖ Your preferences and settings are **stored**
- ‚úÖ You can access everything again when you log back in

**You don't need to manually save before logging out!**

## Important Security Notes

### Why Logging Out Matters

:::warning Security Best Practice
**Always log out when:**
1. **Using shared computers** - Library, conference room, coworker's desk
2. **Public locations** - Coffee shops, airports, hotels
3. **Leaving your desk** - Even briefly in open office spaces
4. **End of workday** - Good security hygiene
:::

### What Logging Out Protects

Logging out prevents:
- ‚ùå Unauthorized access to your conversations
- ‚ùå Others viewing sensitive company information
- ‚ùå Accidental changes to your account
- ‚ùå Data exposure if computer is compromised

## Special Situations

### Session Timeout (Automatic Logout)

**What is it?**
After a period of inactivity, Neurond Assistant automatically logs you out for security.

**Typical timeout:** 30-60 minutes of no activity

**What happens:**
1. Warning appears: "Your session will expire in 5 minutes"
2. You can click "Stay Logged In" to continue
3. If you don't respond, you're logged out automatically
4. Your work is saved, but you need to log in again

[Screenshot: Session timeout warning message]

:::tip Extend Your Session
If you're actively thinking or reading, move your mouse or click somewhere occasionally to keep your session active.
:::

---

### Force Logout (Troubleshooting)

**When to use:**
If you're experiencing issues and normal logout isn't working.

**Steps:**
1. Clear your browser cookies for Neurond Assistant site
2. Close all browser windows completely
3. Reopen browser and navigate to login page

**Alternative:**
1. Close the browser tab
2. Open an incognito/private window
3. Navigate to Neurond Assistant URL
4. You'll need to log in fresh

---

### Logout from Multiple Devices

**If you're logged in on multiple devices:**
- Logging out on one device doesn't log you out of others
- Each device requires separate logout
- For security, logout from all devices individually

**To logout everywhere:**
Some systems offer "Logout from all devices" option:
1. Go to Settings ‚Üí Security
2. Look for "Active Sessions"
3. Click "End all sessions" or "Logout everywhere"

[Screenshot: Active sessions management screen]

## Troubleshooting Logout Issues

### Can't Find Logout Button

**Problem:** No logout option visible

**Solutions:**
- ‚úì Check under profile icon (top-right)
- ‚úì Look in Settings menu
- ‚úì Look in dropdown menus in navigation bar
- ‚úì Try accessing Settings ‚Üí Account ‚Üí Sign Out
- ‚úì Force logout by clearing cookies (see above)

---

### Logout Doesn't Work

**Problem:** Clicking logout does nothing

**Solutions:**
- ‚úì Try a different browser
- ‚úì Clear browser cache and cookies
- ‚úì Close browser completely and reopen
- ‚úì Use incognito mode for next login
- ‚úì Contact IT support if issue persists

---

### Still Seeing Your Data After Logout

**Problem:** Personal information visible after logout

**Solutions:**
- ‚úì Hard refresh the page (Ctrl+F5 or Cmd+Shift+R)
- ‚úì Clear browser cache
- ‚úì Close all browser tabs and windows
- ‚úì Report issue to IT immediately (possible security concern)

## Related Security Topics

### Lock Your Workstation

**Even better than logout:**
When stepping away briefly, lock your computer instead:
- **Windows:** Press Windows key + L
- **Mac:** Press Cmd + Control + Q
- **Chromebook:** Press Search + L

This keeps Neurond Assistant open but requires your password to access.

### Browser Security

**Additional protection:**
- Enable browser's password manager to avoid storing credentials unsafely
- Use different passwords for different systems
- Keep your browser updated
- Enable two-factor authentication if available
